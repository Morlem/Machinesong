Letter Boxer

BACKGROUND

This was a project for the mobile portion of the Basic Interaction Design course at Carnegie Mellon. Our product was designed for those whose bodies may be old, but who are young at heart. With our application they can stay both physically active and socially connected.

EXPLORE

For this team project we abridged the design process by starting with the creation a persona to fit our design challenge. We then generated scenarios of use and sketches to explore the space and finally developed the design that you see in the demo above.

Our persona described Ruth McGrath, a 79 year-old retired actuary that lives in the retirement community of Applewood Estates in Boca Raton, Florida. Her technology use is limited to the TV and to her cell phone on which she’s comfortable making and receiving calls, but she gets help when she wants to add new numbers. In the full persona I described her family life, some history, what she’s up to in 2010, a typical day, and her current goals.

Our early ideas included: a social network designed for seniors who want to stay physically active, an application where seniors could input a personal profile and that would help introduce them to similar minded folk, and an application designed to allow senior citizens of a community to take pictures of problems and send reports to management. Ultimately we realized that it was unrealistic to presume that a typical septuagenarian would be so fluent with technology or be inclined to engage in social networks.

BUILD

After the team built wireframes and mocked up some of the screens, it was my job to build the Flash demo above. Pairing it with the scenario made it easier to communicate key ideas.

LESSONS LEARNED

This was one of the best team experiences I had at Carnegie Mellon. One lesson from this was that if everyone has a good attitude and is willing to work, you can have fun while making something interesting. It also helped that we all had broad enough skill sets that we were able to give useful feedback while still allowing that teammate to own that portion of the project.

Another important takeaway was that when the constraints of a project don’t allow for background research, it’s important to explicitly define intelligent expectations for the consumer’s current technology use and other relevant factors. The idea of an application to facilitate meeting familiar strangers within a community sounds appealing to me, but it probably wouldn't appeal to more 79 year-old retirees.
